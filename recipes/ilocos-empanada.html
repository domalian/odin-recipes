Ilocos Empanada

Original by: Jennifer Segal

What is this dish?

Another small bit from our trustworthy friend, ChatGPT: the Ilocos empanada, a popular street food from the Ilocos region in the Philippines, traces its roots to Spanish colonization when the concept of empanadas was introduced. Over time, the Ilocanos created a unique version using rice flour for the dough, giving it a distinctive orange hue from annatto seeds. The filling typically includes grated green papaya, mung beans, local longganisa (sausage), and sometimes egg. Deep-fried to achieve a crispy exterior, these empanadas are a staple of local street food, especially in Vigan and Laoag, and are celebrated in local festivals. Variations like the larger Batac empanada and the thinner-crusted Vigan empanada highlight regional preferences.

Ingredients (8 servings)

  • 2 cups of rice flour
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 cup of annatto oil
  • 1 cup of ground pork (Filipino longganisa for authenticity)
  • 1 cup of grated green papaya
  • 1/2 cup of grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup of chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup of minced garlic
  • Desired amount of salt and pepper
  • Needed amount of vegetable oil for frying
  • Desired amountof spicy vinegar
  • 4 pieces of eggs

Steps #

  1. Combine rice flour, salt, water, and annatto oil in a mixing bowl
  2. Knead the mixture until dough is smooth and pliable
  3. Divide the dough into small balls, and flatten them into thin, round wrappers
  4. Mix the meat, grated papaya, grated carrots, chopped green onions, minced garlic, salt and pepper into a different bowl to make our filling
  5. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each rice flour wrapper
  6. Crack and place an egg in the center of the filling
  7. Fold the wrapper in half, sealing the edges by pressing them together. Use water to help seal the edges if needed
  8. Make sure the empanadas are tightly sealed to prevent any filling form leaking out during frying
  9. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or pot
  10. Place the empanada in the oil once it is hot
  11. Fry until they turn crispy (usually around 5-7 minutes)
  12. Remove the empanadas from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil. Be careful as the empanadas may stick to the paper towels as it cools off
  13. Serve hot with spicy vinegar
